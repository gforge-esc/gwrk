# Naming Forge

Pressure-forge names and category definitions through argument, beauty, and collision.

## When This Fires

Product naming, CLI command naming, category definition, taxonomy design, or any definitional work where language is the deliverable.

## The Three Passes

### Pass 1 — Dialectical

For the proposed name or category:

**Thesis**: Build the strongest case for this name. What does it communicate? What does it evoke? What does it own?

**Antithesis**: Build the strongest case against it. What does it confuse? What does it collide with? What does it fail to communicate?

**Synthesis**: What survives the collision? What is the refined position?

> ARGUE BOTH SIDES WITH FULL COMMITMENT, THEN RESOLVE THE TENSION INTO A SINGLE POSITION.

### Pass 2 — Aesthetic

Evaluate the surviving candidates purely on elegance:

- **Mouth feel**: Is it satisfying to say aloud?
- **Visual weight**: Does it look right in a terminal, a logo, a URL?
- **Cognitive load**: Can someone hear it once and spell it?
- **Longevity**: Will this name embarrass you in 5 years?
- **Namespace**: Does it own its Google result? Its npm/crate namespace?

> WHAT'S THE MOST BEAUTIFUL VERSION OF THIS? OPTIMIZE FOR ELEGANCE OVER EFFICIENCY.

Score each candidate 1-10 across these dimensions.

### Pass 3 — Combinatorial

Force-connect the naming domain with an unrelated domain to break default thinking:

- Apply [music theory] to [your naming problem]. What emerges?
- Apply [architecture] to [your taxonomy]. What structure appears?
- Apply [biology] to [your category]. What taxonomy feels natural?

> APPLY [X] PRINCIPLES TO [Y]. WHAT UNEXPECTED SOLUTIONS EMERGE FROM THAT COLLISION?

This pass exists to break the "obvious" name and find the one that feels **inevitable** in hindsight.

## Output Contract

```markdown
## Naming Assessment: [context]

### Candidates
| Name | Thesis Score | Antithesis Damage | Aesthetic (1-10) | Namespace Clear |
|------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|----------------|

### Dialectical Winner
[name] — [synthesis rationale]

### Combinatorial Wild Card
[unexpected candidate from domain collision] — [why it works]

### Recommendation
[final pick] — [one-sentence justification]
```

Reasoning Modes

(How to think before answering)

1. **Analytical** - Break down, dissect, and find structure.
    
    > "BREAK DOWN TO ITS CORE STRUCTURE. WHAT ARE THE COMPONENTS AND HOW DO THEY RELATE?"
    
2. **Synthetic** - Connect disparate ideas into unified framework.
    
    > "WHAT DO THESE THINGS HAVE IN COMMON AT A DEEPER LEVEL? BUILD ME A UNIFIED FRAMEWORK."
    
3. **Dialectical** - Thesis, antithesis, synthesis.
    
    > "ARGUE BOTH SIDES WITH FULL COMMITMENT, THEN RESOLVE THE TENSION INTO A SINGLE POSITION."
    
4. **Forensic** - Work backward from outcomes to causes.
    
    > "THIS FAILED. WORK BACKWARD AND IDENTIFY THE ROOT CAUSES, NOT THE SYMPTOMS."
    
5. **Probabilistic** - Weight uncertainty explicitly across claims.
    
    > "GIVE YOUR CONFIDENCE LEVEL ON EACH. SEPARATE WHAT YOU KNOW FROM WHAT YOU'RE INFERRING."
    
6. **Counterfactual** - Explore what didn't happen.
    
    > "WHAT IF [X] HAD NEVER EXISTED? TRACE THE ALTERNATE PATH FORWARD."
    
7. **Reductive** - Strip to first principles. Remove everything else.
    
    > "IGNORE CONVENTIONAL WISDOM. REMOVE INHERITED ASSUMPTIONS. START FROM GROUND TRUTH ONLY."
    
8. **Expansive** - Broaden before narrowing.
    
    > "GENERATE MAXIMUM SURFACE AREA BEFORE FILTERING. VOLUME FIRST, JUDGMENT SECOND."

Evaluative Modes

(Finding the cracks before they find you)

1. **Adversarial** - Attack the idea. Find every crack.
    
    > "YOU'RE A SKEPTIC WHO NEEDS TO BE CONVINCED. ATTACK THIS WITH EVERYTHING YOU HAVE."
    
2. **Steel-man** - Build the strongest argument before critiquing.
    
    > "BUILD THE STRONGEST POSSIBLE VERSION OF THE OPPOSING ARGUMENT BEFORE YOU RESPOND."
    
3. **Pre-mortem** - Assume failure. Work backward to causes.
    
    > "THIS FAILED 6 MONTHS FROM NOW. WHAT WERE THE MOST LIKELY CAUSES?"
    
4. **Audit** - Systematic assumption mapping.
    
    > "LIST EVERY ASSUMPTION THIS DEPENDS ON. FOR EACH ONE, WHAT BREAKS IF IT'S WRONG?"
    
5. **Comparative** - Evaluate against alternatives, not in isolation.
    
    > "DON'T EVALUATE THIS ALONE. COMPARE IT AGAINST THE 3 MOST VIABLE ALTERNATIVES."
    
6. **Calibration** - Assess confidence levels across components.
    
    > "RATE YOUR CONFIDENCE 1-10 ON EACH SECTION. WHERE ARE YOU MOST LIKELY TO BE WRONG?"

Creative Modes

(Breaking the default answer)

1. **Generative** - Raw ideation without filtering.
    
    > "NO FILTERING. GIVE ME 15 IDEAS INCLUDING THE ONES THAT SEEM TOO WEIRD."
    
2. **Combinatorial** - Force-connect two unrelated domains.
    
    > "APPLY [X] PRINCIPLES TO [Y]. WHAT UNEXPECTED SOLUTIONS EMERGE FROM THAT COLLISION?"
    
3. **Subversive** - Deliberately violate the obvious approach.
    
    > "WHAT'S THE LEAST EXPECTED SOLUTION THAT STILL ACTUALLY WORKS?"
    
4. **Aesthetic** - Optimize for elegance, not just correctness.
    
    > "WHAT'S THE MOST BEAUTIFUL VERSION OF THIS? OPTIMIZE FOR ELEGANCE OVER EFFICIENCY."
    
5. **Narrative** - Frame everything as story. Useful for persuasion.
    
    > "TURN THIS INTO A STORY ARC. WHAT'S THE TENSION, THE TURN, THE RESOLUTION?"
    
6. **Metaphorical** - Explain exclusively through analogy.
    
    > "EXPLAIN THIS USING ONLY METAPHORS AND ANALOGIES. NO DIRECT DESCRIPTION ALLOWED."

Persona Modes

(Decide who's in the room)

1. **Domain Expert** - Vertical knowledge, 0 vagueness tolerance.
    
    > "YOU ARE A 25-YEAR VETERAN IN THIS FIELD. NO HEDGING. NO BASICS. TALK TO ME LIKE A PEER."
    
2. **Skeptic** - Default distrust. Requires evidence for every claim.
    
    > "ASSUME EVERY CLAIM NEEDS PROOF. FLAG ANYTHING I CAN'T SUBSTANTIATE."
    
3. **Naive Outsider** - Surfaces what experts stop seeing.
    
    > "APPROACH WITH ZERO CONTEXT. WHAT WOULD SOMEONE BRAND NEW NOTICE THAT EXPERTS MISSED?"
    
4. **Practitioner** - Optimizes for execution, not theory.
    
    > "FORGET WHAT'S THEORETICALLY CORRECT. WHAT ACTUALLY GETS EXECUTED IN THE REAL WORLD?"
    
5. **Historian** - Pattern-matches across long time horizons.
    
    > "WHEN HAS THIS HAPPENED BEFORE? WHAT DID THOSE CASES HAVE IN COMMON?"
    
6. **Futurist** - Extrapolates second and third-order effects.
    
    > "WHAT HAPPENS AFTER THE OBVIOUS OUTCOME? TRACE THE CASCADE 3 STEPS FORWARD."
    
7. **Operator** - Turns strategy into specific next actions.
    
    > "TURN THIS STRATEGY INTO SPECIFIC NEXT ACTIONS. WHAT HAPPENS MONDAY MORNING?"
    
8. **Devil's Advocate** - Opposes you regardless of merit.
    
    > "ARGUE AGAINST MY POSITION WITH FULL CONVICTION. DON'T CONCEDE ANYTHING."

Communication Modes

(Change how the answer lands)

1. **Teacher** - Builds understanding from the ground up.
    
    > "EXPLAIN THIS FROM FIRST PRINCIPLES. MEET ME WHERE I AM. USE ONE ANALOGY PER CONCEPT."
    
2. **Peer** - Lateral thinking partner. No hierarchy.
    
    > "TALK TO ME LIKE A PEER. CHALLENGE ME. BUILD ON WHAT I'M SAYING."
    
3. **Editor** - Improves without changing voice or intent.
    
    > "IMPROVE THIS WITHOUT CHANGING MY VOICE OR INTENT. MAKE IT SHARPER, NOT DIFFERENT."
    
4. **Translator** - Converts the complex into simple.
    
    > "TRANSLATE THIS FOR SOMEONE SMART BUT COMPLETELY OUTSIDE THE FIELD."
    
5. **Interviewer** - Asks questions instead of giving answers.
    
    > "DON'T ANSWER YET. ASK ME THE QUESTIONS THAT WOULD GET YOU TO A BETTER ANSWER."
    
6. **Synthesizer** - Compresses information into essential signal.
    
    > "COMPRESS THIS INTO THE ESSENTIAL SIGNAL. WHAT ABSOLUTELY MUST SURVIVE THE CUT?"

Operational Modes

(Moving from thinking to doing)

1. **Planning** - Sequence, dependencies, allocation, timeline.
    
    > "BUILD ME A SEQUENCED PLAN WITH DEPENDENCIES FLAGGED AND CRITICAL PATH IDENTIFIED."
    
2. **Bugging** - Systematic isolation of what's broken and why.
    
    > "ISOLATE WHAT'S BROKEN. WORK THROUGH IT SYSTEMATICALLY UNTIL YOU FIND THE ROOT CAUSE."
    
3. **Optimizing** - Identify marginal gains.
    
    > "THIS WORKS. NOW MAKE IT 30% BETTER WITHOUT CHANGING WHAT MAKES IT WORK."
    
4. **Stress-Testing** - Apply pressure to find failure points.
    
    > "APPLY MAXIMUM PRESSURE. WHERE DOES THIS BREAK FIRST?"
    
5. **Prioritizing** - Forced ranking with explicit tradeoff articulation.
    
    > "RANK THESE. NOW JUSTIFY THE BOTTOM. NOW TELL ME WHY THE TOP COULD BE WRONG."
    
6. **Modeling** - Simulate how a person/system/market responds.
    
    > "SIMULATE HOW [PERSON\SYSTEM\MARKET] WOULD RESPOND TO THIS. BE SPECIFIC."

Meta Modes

(Prompting the prompting)

1. **Socratic** - Answer every question with a better question.
    
    > "DON'T ANSWER YET. ASK ME THE QUESTION THAT GETS CLOSER TO THE REAL ANSWER."
    
2. **Recursive** - Apply the same analysis to itself.
    
    > "NOW CRITIQUE THAT CRITIQUE. APPLY THE SAME PRESSURE TO YOUR OWN REASONING."
    
3. **Uncertainty** - Separate known, inferred, and assumed.
    
    > "SEPARATE WHAT YOU KNOW, WHAT YOU'RE INFERRING, AND WHAT YOU'RE ASSUMING. LABEL EACH."
    
4. **Contrarian** - Default to the opposite of consensus.
    
    > "TAKE THE OPPOSITE POSITION OF WHATEVER THE CONSENSUS IS. DEFEND IT SERIOUSLY."
    
5. **Integrative** - Unify outputs from multiple modes.
    
    > "SYNTHESIZE EVERYTHING WE'VE COVERED INTO A SINGLE COHERENT FRAMEWORK. NO REDUNDANCY."

How to Use These

What to Know

- Every conversation you have with an LLM activates a 'mode' whether you choose one or not.
    
- The default is Analytical. It's the safest (and also the most averaged) one.
    
- You can 10x your output quality by doing the following:
    
    1. Pick the mode that matches what the problem actually needs.
        
    2. Name it at the start of your prompt.
        
    3. Stack two or three for decisions that matter.
        
- [Creative problems: Generative + Subversive + Aesthetic] [High-stakes decisions: Forensic + Audit + Pre-mortem]
    
- Be deliberate.
    

How to Stack Modes

- A single mental model applied to every problem is the same mistake as using one tool for every job.
    
- Instead, try the following...
    
- **If you are creating something:**
    
    - [Generative + Subversive + Aesthetic]
        
    - This creates volume first and violates the obvious. Then it optimizes for elegance.
        
- **If you are making a high-stakes decision:**
    
    - [Forensic + Audit + Pre-mortem]
        
    - This works backward from failure and maps every assumption. Then it assumes it will fail anyway.
        
- **If you are communicating something difficult:**
    
    - [Adversarial + Editor + Calibration]
        
    - This attacks your own argument and sharpens what survives. Then it rates confidence in each section.
        
- **If you are learning something fast:**
    
    - [Reductive + Metaphorical + Teacher]
        
    - This strips to ground level of truth and translates it through analogy.
        
    - Then it explains it to someone who knows nothing.
        

Ways to Setup

1. The Manual Method

- Insert the prompt directly into your message. Type the mode name before your question.
    
- > "Use Forensic mode. This campaign failed last quarter. Work backward and identify the root causes."
    

2. The System Prompt Method

- Put your most-used modes into Claude's Custom Instructions Tell it which modes to run by default and when to switch.
    
- Every conversation inherits it automatically and you never have to think about it again.
    

3. The Shortcut Method

- Create a running note/doc with your favorite mode stacks.
    
- One tap to copy and paste at the start of any session.
    
- It's faster than typing from scratch and faster than rebuilding context every time.
